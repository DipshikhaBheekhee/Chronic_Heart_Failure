User: How can I tell if my leg swelling is serious?


Chatbot: I’m here to support you, but I need to remind you that I’m not a medical professional.

Leg swelling can be a concerning symptom of heart failure, and it’s essential to determine its severity. Here are some guidelines from trusted Canadian health organizations like the Heart & Stroke Foundation and the Ottawa Heart Institute:

If you notice swelling in your legs or feet, pay attention to its severity:
Mild swelling: You may feel a bit tightness or discomfort, but you can still move around comfortably.
Moderate swelling: Your legs or feet might be slightly puffy, making it harder to fit into shoes. You may experience some pain or discomfort when standing or walking.
Severe swelling: Your legs or feet are severely swollen, making it difficult to move around. You may feel short of breath, dizzy, or lightheaded.
Look for other signs that might indicate serious leg swelling:
Sudden increase in swelling (e.g., within a day or two)
Swelling that worsens over time
Pain or discomfort in your legs or feet
Redness, warmth, or discoloration around the affected area
If you experience any of these symptoms, please contact your doctor or care team immediately.
